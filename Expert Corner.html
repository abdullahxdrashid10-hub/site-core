Surface Preparation

Ensure surfaces are clean, dry, and level before tile installation.

Installation · Step 1

Surface Preparation

The single most important step in any tile job is what happens before a single tile is laid. The substrate must be structurally sound, completely dry, and level to within 3mm over a 2-metre span. Any existing paint, grease, or loose material must be fully removed — a bonding primer applied afterward ensures the adhesive grips properly. For wet areas like bathrooms, apply a waterproof membrane and allow it to cure fully before proceeding. Skipping this step is the number one cause of tile failure within the first year of installation.

Correct Adhesive Usage

Use high-quality tile adhesive suitable for wall or floor applications.

Installation · Step 2

Correct Adhesive Usage

Not all adhesives are equal — using the wrong type is a costly mistake. Wall tiles require a non-slip adhesive that holds the tile in place while it sets; floor tiles need a heavy-duty flexible adhesive that can handle foot traffic and thermal movement. For large-format tiles over 60x60cm, always use a full-bed application method — spread adhesive on both the substrate and the back of the tile to eliminate any air pockets. Work in small sections of no more than 1 square metre at a time, and never let the adhesive skin over before placing the tile.

Tile Spacing Techniques

Maintain proper tile spacing for balanced alignment and smooth grout finishing.

Installation · Step 3

Tile Spacing Techniques

Consistent spacing is what separates an amateur finish from a professional one. Always use plastic tile spacers — 2mm for wall tiles, 3–5mm for floor tiles — and check alignment with a spirit level every three or four tiles. Before committing to adhesive, do a dry lay of the entire area to plan your cuts and ensure no tile at the edge is smaller than half its width. Grout joint width also affects the visual weight of the installation: tighter joints (1–2mm) give a sleek seamless look, while wider joints (5mm+) suit rustic or handmade tiles that have slight size variations baked into their character.
