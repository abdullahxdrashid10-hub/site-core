Tile Guides

Step-by-step guides for tile size selection, glossy vs matte comparison, wall vs floor tiles, and choosing tiles according to lighting.

Tile Size Selection

Learn how to choose the perfect tile size according to your space.

Size Guide

Tile Size Selection

Large format tiles (60×60cm+) visually expand smaller rooms by minimising grout lines. Medium tiles (30×30cm) are the go-to for kitchens and bathrooms, while mosaic tiles add texture to feature walls and niches. Always consider room proportions — oversized tiles in a compact space can feel heavy and mismatched.

Glossy vs Matte Tiles

Compare glossy and matte finishes for durability, shine & cleaning.

Finish Guide

Glossy vs Matte Tiles

Glossy tiles reflect light beautifully, making spaces feel brighter and larger — perfect for walls. Matte tiles resist slipping and hide smudges far better, making them the safer choice for floors and high-traffic areas. Satin finishes offer a middle ground with a subtle sheen that's easy to maintain day-to-day.

Wall Tiles vs Floor Tiles

Understand the difference between wall tiles and floor tiles.

Usage Guide

Wall Tiles vs Floor Tiles

Floor tiles must carry slip resistance ratings (R9–R13) and withstand heavy foot traffic — they're denser and thicker by design. Wall tiles are lighter and thinner, prioritising aesthetics. Using wall tiles on a floor is unsafe. However, floor tiles can be used on walls with the right adhesive and support system.

Lighting & Tile Selection

Discover how lighting changes tile color, texture & appearance.

Lighting Guide

Lighting & Tile Selection

Natural daylight brings out the true tone of a tile — warm whites turn ivory under incandescent bulbs. Textured or 3D tiles cast dramatic shadows under directional lighting, enhancing depth. For showrooms or samples, always evaluate tiles under the same lighting conditions as your actual room before purchasing.

Tile Patterns & Layouts

Explore herringbone, subway, checkerboard & modern tile layouts.

Pattern Guide

Tile Patterns & Layouts

Herringbone creates a dynamic, high-end look ideal for entryways and kitchens. Subway layouts are timeless and versatile. Checkerboard patterns add a retro-chic character to bathrooms. Large diagonal layouts make rooms feel wider. The right pattern can completely transform a space — even with the same tile.

Kitchen Backsplash Guide

Stylish backsplash tile ideas for modern and luxury kitchens.

Kitchen Guide

Kitchen Backsplash Guide

A backsplash protects walls from grease and moisture while serving as a design focal point. Glossy metro tiles keep it classic; Zellige or handmade tiles add artisan warmth. For luxury kitchens, full-slab porcelain or marble-effect tiles create a seamless, magazine-worthy finish. Always seal natural stone thoroughly.
