Latest Kitchen Tile Trends

Modern tile trends & stylish textures.

Trends · 2026

Latest Kitchen Tile Trends

2026 is the year of raw elegance. Large-format porcelain slabs in warm greige tones are dominating modern kitchens, replacing the traditional small-tile backsplash. Fluted textures — once reserved for furniture — are now appearing on kitchen walls, adding depth without the need for colour. Zellige tiles from Morocco are having a massive revival, prized for their handmade imperfections and rich glazed surfaces. If you want one investment piece, go for a statement backsplash in a bold geometric pattern — it anchors the room instantly.

Small Kitchen Ideas

Space-saving tile combinations.

Design · Space

Small Kitchen Ideas

The right tile can visually double the size of a small kitchen. Start with the floor — large light-coloured tiles laid diagonally create an illusion of width. On the walls, vertical stacked subway tiles draw the eye upward, making low ceilings feel taller. Avoid busy patterns in tight spaces; instead, let texture do the work — a subtle linen-effect porcelain is refined without being overwhelming. Mirror-finish tiles behind the hob bounce light beautifully and keep the space feeling open and airy.

Modern vs Classic

Compare modern & classic styles.

Style · Guide

Modern vs Classic

Modern tiles embrace minimalism — think concrete-look surfaces, matte finishes, and a strictly neutral palette of whites, greys, and blacks. They work best in open-plan layouts where the tile becomes part of the architecture itself. Classic styles, on the other hand, favour marble veining, ornate encaustic patterns, and warm ivory or terracotta tones. They bring warmth and history to a space. The emerging trend in 2026? Blending both — a classic marble floor paired with flat matte wall tiles creates an unexpectedly sophisticated contrast.

Color Combination Ideas

Best kitchen tile color palettes.

Colour · Palette

Color Combination Ideas

Colour in a kitchen needs to work with light, not against it. North-facing kitchens benefit from warm creams and soft terracottas that compensate for cool natural light. South-facing spaces can handle bolder choices — deep forest green tiles or midnight blue are stunning when bathed in sunlight. For a timeless pairing, try warm white walls with a charcoal floor tile — the contrast is sharp but never cold. If you want colour without commitment, use it on the island base only, keeping the perimeter walls neutral. Sage green remains the most searched tile colour heading into 2026.

Budget Renovation

Affordable renovation solutions.

Budget · Tips

Budget Renovation

A full kitchen retile doesn't have to break the bank. The single highest-impact change you can make is replacing the backsplash — it's a small area but the most visible surface in a kitchen. Peel-and-stick tiles have improved dramatically in quality and now closely mimic real ceramic and stone. For flooring, consider large-format vinyl tiles — they're waterproof, durable, and a fraction of the cost of porcelain. If you do splurge on one real tile, make it the hob surround. That single statement piece will elevate the entire room without the cost of a full reno.

Luxury Kitchen Designs

Premium tile inspiration ideas.

Luxury · Inspiration

Luxury Kitchen Designs

In a luxury kitchen, the tile is the architecture. Bookmatched marble slabs — where two adjacent cuts of stone mirror each other — create a dramatic, gallery-like effect on the wall behind the range. Unlacquered brass fixtures paired with aged terracotta floor tiles bring organic warmth to an otherwise sleek space. For the ultimate statement, consider a full-height backsplash in Calacatta Gold marble running seamlessly from counter to ceiling. The key to luxury tiling is restraint — one extraordinary material, used with confidence, always outperforms a mix of five merely expensive ones.
